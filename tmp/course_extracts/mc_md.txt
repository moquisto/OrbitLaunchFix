What is Monte Carlo simulation?
‚ÄĘMC is any algorithm based on random numbers
‚ÄĘUsed for calculating multiple integrals, generate 
probability distributions, growth phenomena, etc
‚ÄĘResults are obtained as averages over random samples
‚ÄĘMC can be exact in the sense systematic-error-free
‚ÄĘResults are exact up to statistical errors
‚ÄĘBetter than deterministic methods 
for large number of degrees of freedom

MD is chaotic

equilibrium and/or dynamic properties.

Thermostats in MD
‚ÄĘAndersen thermostat: Every n-th step (n is a parameter):
‚ÄĘchoose a particle at random or loop over all atoms
‚ÄĘre-assign random velocities from a Boltzmann distribution,  
take vx/y/z from Gaussian distribution with ŌÉ2 = kBT/m , őľ = 0, m =1
‚ÄĘAdvantage: very simple to implement Disadvantage: strong 
disruption of dynamics 
‚ÄĘBussi thermostat:
‚ÄĘScale the velocities and add noise to the velocities
‚ÄĘNos√©-Hoover thermostat:
‚ÄĘAdd the kinetic energy as a degree of freedom in the Hamiltonian 
Advantage: smaller disruption of dynamics 
‚ÄĘDisadvantage: can be tricky to implement and control

Radial Distribution Function
‚ÄĘThe structure of a liquid is difÔ¨Ācult to analyze, 
for N atoms you have a 3N-dimensional system
‚ÄĘY ou can look at low-dimensional distributions:
‚ÄĘg(1)(x), the probability to Ô¨Ānd a particle at position x: 
useful for a crystal, uniform for a liquid
‚ÄĘg(2)(r), the radial distribution function, which gives 
 the probability of Ô¨Ānding a particle at distance r 
 from another particle, relative to an ideal gas
‚ÄĘCalculating g(r), which is essentially a histogram normalized by r (in 2D):
‚ÄĘChoose a bin width and make a list of bins up to a certain maximum distance
‚ÄĘFor one or more conÔ¨Āgurations do a loop over all particles pairs (which are 
N(N-1)/2); for each pair determine the distance and add 1 to the 
corresponding bin
‚ÄĘg(r)= bin_count(r) / (shell_area x #conf. x rho x N/2), where the shell_area = 
2 pi ((r+binwith)2 - r2) and rho is the particle density
