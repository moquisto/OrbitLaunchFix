What is Monte Carlo simulation?
•MC is any algorithm based on random numbers
•Used for calculating multiple integrals, generate 
probability distributions, growth phenomena, etc
•Results are obtained as averages over random samples
•MC can be exact in the sense systematic-error-free
•Results are exact up to statistical errors
•Better than deterministic methods 
for large number of degrees of freedom

Ensembles in statistical mechanics
•Microcanonical ensemble: system isolated from environment, 
energy E=constant, particle number N=constant 
•Canonical ensemble: divide a microcanonical ensemble into 
two parts, called the ”system” and the ”heat bath or 
reservoir”, that can exchange energy but not particles.  
E≠constant, instead T=constant
•Grand canonical ensemble: the system and reservoir can 
exchange energy and particles.

===== PAGE 28 =====

Thermostats in MD
•Andersen thermostat: Every n-th step (n is a parameter):
•choose a particle at random or loop over all atoms
•re-assign random velocities from a Boltzmann distribution,  
take vx/y/z from Gaussian distribution with σ2 = kBT/m , μ = 0, m =1
•Advantage: very simple to implement Disadvantage: strong 
disruption of dynamics 
•Bussi thermostat:
•Scale the velocities and add noise to the velocities
•Nosé-Hoover thermostat:
•Add the kinetic energy as a degree of freedom in the Hamiltonian 
Advantage: smaller disruption of dynamics 
•Disadvantage: can be tricky to implement and control

===== PAGE 33 =====

Radial Distribution Function
•The structure of a liquid is difﬁcult to analyze, 
for N atoms you have a 3N-dimensional system
•Y ou can look at low-dimensional distributions:
•g(1)(x), the probability to ﬁnd a particle at position x: 
useful for a crystal, uniform for a liquid
•g(2)(r), the radial distribution function, which gives 
 the probability of ﬁnding a particle at distance r 
 from another particle, relative to an ideal gas
•Calculating g(r), which is essentially a histogram normalized by r (in 2D):
•Choose a bin width and make a list of bins up to a certain maximum distance
•For one or more conﬁgurations do a loop over all particles pairs (which are 
N(N-1)/2); for each pair determine the distance and add 1 to the 
corresponding bin
•g(r)= bin_count(r) / (shell_area x #conf. x rho x N/2), where the shell_area = 
2 pi ((r+binwith)2 - r2) and rho is the particle density
example: g(r) of a LJ liquid
