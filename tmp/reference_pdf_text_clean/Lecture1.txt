3 Design of aircraft ground steering control
system based on control continuation method
To change the unstable dynamic behavior in the nonlinear
aircraft rollout and steering system to a stable state, three
nose wheel steering angle feedback control systems are de-
signed based on the control continuation method. In addition,
the influences of the linear and nonlinear feedback con-
trollers on the system bifurcation characteristics are in-
vestigated.
3.1 Linear feedback controller
The aircraft nose wheel steering linear feedback control
system is designed as illustrated in Figure 5 . In Figure 5 ,kis
the gain coefficient of the feedback controller, δlis the nose
wheel steering control signal, r0is the expected yawing an-
gular acceleration, and δris the error of the expected and the
actual yawing angular acceleration after amplification. The
expected control signal δ0is set as the bifurcation parameter,
and the one-parameter bifurcation curves for various values
of the linear control parameter kare shown in Figure 6 .
As can be seen in Figure 6 , when the linear feedback
control parameter is k= 2, an unstable periodic turning
phenomenon will take place as the expected steering control
signal is located both in δ0∈[0.1,0.27] when the aircraft
cornering speed is lower than 60 m/s and in δ0∈[0.39,0.96]
over the whole speed domain. With the decrease in the
control parameter k, the Hopf bifurcation phenomenon is
delayed along the positive direction of the steering control
signal δ0. When k≤ 1, the number of the Hopf bifurcation
points decreases to one when δ0 is within the range of
δ0∈[0,1.5]. Additionally, the stable solution curves become
longer so that the stable region area increases. As the control
parameter kdrops to 0.1, the Hopf bifurcation phenomenon
disappears. Thus the aircraft could always roll out along a
circle at a constant velocity stably over the whole parameter
domain, indicating that the linear feedback controller can
efficiently improve the aircraft rollout directional stability of
the nonlinear turning dynamic system.

AbstractAircraft steering instability during the taxiing process sometimes occurs resulting from the unstable bifurcation
phenomena of the nonlinear steering dynamic system, which may lead to severe accidents. In this study, the control parameter
design of an aircraft nose wheel steering control system based on the control continuation method is proposed to reduce the
number of unstable bifurcation points and enlarge the system stable region area in a certain parameter domain. Thus, the aircraft
turning performance can be improved greatly. First, a nonlinear aircraft rollout dynamic model is constructed, and the aircraft
ground steering bifurcation characteristics are obtained under the open-loop steering control system. Then, the bifurcation
performances under three closed-loop nose wheel steering controllers based on the feedback control method are compared, and
the control parameter of the quadratic nonlinear controller with a better bifurcation control effect is selected as a second
bifurcation parameter to analyze the directional system stability via two-parameter bifurcation analysis. Further, the influence
of a crosswind on the aircraft ground steering bifurcation performance is studied for various control parameter values. Then, the
control effects of a traditional proportional-integral-derivative (PID) nose wheel steering control law and the designed feedback
controller are compared and analyzed. Finally, a semi-physical experiment of the aircraft nose wheel steering system is carried
out, and different control parameter values are selected for test verification, which can further verify the control effect of the
designed nonlinear feedback controller. The results indicate that the feedback control method is unable to vary the system
bifurcation types, but appropriate control parameter design can effectively shrink the system instability region and even
eliminate the unstable Hopf bifurcation points under some specific working conditions, improving the aircraft ground turning
stability remarkably from the perspective of the global parameter region.
Keywordsaircraft taxiing dynamics and stability, nose wheel steering control system, control continuation, steering bi-
furcation characteristics, quadratic feedback control

portant stages during the whole flight process [1], where

during the running phase sometimes results from the bi-
furcation phenomena of the nonlinear system, which may
cause the aircraft to spin on the runway or veer off course,
causing severe accidents to occur [4]. The strong non-
linearity and the coupling effects between the structure and
the control systems of the aircraft rollout dynamic system
aggravate the problems of aircraft taxiing maneuvering in-
